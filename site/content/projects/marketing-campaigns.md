## The Problem
After running multiple campaigns, it’s not always clear which ones are actually effective or how customer behavior changes over time. I wanted to understand how engagement builds across campaigns and what drives customers to respond.

## The Data
A marketing campaign dataset simulating real-world campaign performance, including customer demographics, campaign exposure, and response behavior across six campaigns.

## The Analysis
- Compared engagement and response rates across six campaigns  
- Tracked how customer responses evolved over time  
- Analyzed distribution of engagement across the customer base  
- Evaluated spending patterns between responders and non-responders  
- Segmented response behavior by age group  

## Key Insights
- Engagement increased across campaigns, suggesting consistency builds awareness and response over time  
- A small segment of customers drove the majority of engagement  
- Cumulative responses continued to grow, indicating conversions compound rather than plateau  
- Customers who engaged tended to spend less on average, raising questions about discount-driven behavior  
- The 36–50 age group showed the strongest and most consistent response patterns
