## The Problem
School performance is often discussed at a high level, but outcomes can vary significantly across schools and districts. I wanted to explore where those differences exist and what factors may be contributing to them.

## The Data
Massachusetts public school data (2017), including graduation rates, college attendance, class size, and standardized test performance.

## The Analysis
- Compared graduation rates across high schools to identify performance variation  
- Analyzed the relationship between class size, economic disadvantage, and college admission rates  
- Evaluated district-level math performance to highlight early academic gaps  
- Built Tableau dashboard to explore patterns across schools and districts  

## Key Insights
- Graduation rates vary significantly across schools, not evenly distributed across the system  
- Higher levels of economic disadvantage are associated with lower college admission rates  
- Class size alone does not explain performance differences—other factors play a role  
- District-level math performance reveals consistent gaps in early academic outcomes
